%% LEGAL CITATION PACKAGE (legalcite.sty)
%% ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
%%
%% Implements the Canadian Guide to Uniform Legal Citation (McGill Guide),
%% 9th edition.
%%
%% HOW IT WORKS
%% ─────────────
%% 1. Declare all sources in the preamble (below).
%% 2. In the body, cite with \legal{key} or \legal[pinpoint]{key}.
%%    Each call inserts a footnote that is automatically formatted as:
%%      • Full citation + [Short]  — first use of that key
%%      • Short at pinpoint        — subsequent non-consecutive use
%%      • Ibid  (or Ibid at …)    — consecutive same-key use
%%    Case short forms are automatically italicised (McGill Guide §3.5).
%% 3. Use \legalname{key} or \legalshort{key} to print the source name
%%    in running text without generating a footnote.
%% 4. Call \printlegalbibliography at the end to print the Table of Authorities
%%    organised as: Legislation / Case Law / Monographs / Articles.
%%
%% PINPOINT SYNTAX (McGill Guide §1.3)
%%   Cases     → para 32 | paras 27-31 | paras 18, 23
%%   Books     → p 42    | pp 148-155  | pp 214, 218
%%   Articles  → p 5     | pp 5-7
